MIT's Governance Lab explores how technology-related interventions reshape political participation and activism around the world.
GovLab joins MIT researchers with innovative non-profits and corporations that seek to improve the connection between governments and their constituents.
GovLab partners currently include portfolio organizations of the Omidyar Network and the World Bank's Global Partnership for Social Accountability (GPSA).
Lily L. Tsai is an Associate Professor of Political Science at MIT.
Her research focuses on issues of accountability, governance, and political participation in developing countries with a particular emphasis on Chinese politics. Her book, Accountability Without Democracy: Solidary Groups and Public Goods Provision in Rural China, received the 2007-08 Dogan Award from the Society of Comparative Research for the best book published in the field of comparative research.

Tsai is a graduate of Stanford University, where she graduated with honors and distinction in English literature and international relations. She received a M.A. in political science from the University of California, Berkeley, and a Ph.D. in government from Harvard University in 2004. She is the recipient of fellowships from the Fulbright program and the Harvard Academy for International and Area Studies.
